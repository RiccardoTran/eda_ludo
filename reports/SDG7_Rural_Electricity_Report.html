Key Findings

Main Observations

  • Rural electricity access in SSA averages only around 35%
  • Many countries have less than 20% rural electrification
  • The urban-rural gap is approximately 45 percentage points
  • Progress is being made but at a slower pace than urban areas
  • Some countries have made significant strides in rural electrification

Why Rural Electricity Matters

  • Health clinics cannot refrigerate vaccines without power
  • Schools cannot use computers or lights for evening classes
  • Water pumps often require electricity
  • Economic opportunities are limited without power
  • Nearly half of SSA's population lives in rural areas

Data Notes

  • Data shows percentage access only, not quality of electricity service
  • Some countries have missing data for certain years
  • Rural definitions may vary between countries
