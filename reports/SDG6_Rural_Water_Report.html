Overview

What This Report Shows

This report examines access to clean water in villages and rural areas across Sub-Saharan Africa. Rural water access is a critical development challenge affecting health, education, and economic opportunity.

What is "At Least Basic" Water?

  • Water from an improved source (protected well, borehole, rainwater, etc.)
  • Collection time is 30 minutes or less for a round trip

Key Findings

Main Observations

  • Rural water access in SSA averages around 55%
  • This is higher than rural electricity access (~35%) but lower than urban water (~85%)
  • Significant variation exists between countries
  • Access has been improving over the monitoring period
  • Some countries still have very low rural water coverage

Why Rural Water Access Matters

  • Prevents waterborne diseases (cholera, dysentery, typhoid)
  • Reduces time spent collecting water (often hours per day)
  • Women and girls disproportionately bear the burden of water collection
  • Children who collect water miss school hours
  • Clean water is essential for health, education, and economic development
