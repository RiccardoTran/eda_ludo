2. Correlation Analysis

What is Correlation?

Correlation (r) measures how strongly two things are related:

  • r = 0.7-1.0: Strong relationship
  • r = 0.4-0.6: Moderate relationship
  • r = 0.1-0.3: Weak relationship

3. Outlier Detection

Methods Used

  • Z-Score: Values more than 2.5 standard deviations from mean

Main Conclusions

  1. Strong Correlation: Electricity and water access are strongly correlated (r ~0.7-0.8). Countries improving in one tend to improve in the other.
  2. Gap Pattern Similar: The urban-rural inequality pattern is similar for both services - countries with large electricity gaps also have large water gaps.
  3. Rural Electricity is Furthest Behind: Of all 4 indicators, rural electricity has the lowest average (~35%).
  4. Water Generally Better: Both urban and rural water access exceed their electricity counterparts.
  5. Large Country Variation: Some countries have near-universal access; others are still struggling with very low coverage.

Causation Caveat

While electricity and water access are strongly correlated, we cannot conclude that one causes the other. More likely explanations:

  • Both are driven by overall economic development
  • Good governance improves both services
  • Infrastructure investment tends to be comprehensive
  • International development programs often target both

Key Population Findings

  • Nigeria has the largest absolute number of people without electricity and water due to its massive population (~220M)
  • DRC and Ethiopia follow with tens of millions without access
  • Rural areas account for the majority of people without access to both services
  • Even countries with high percentage access may have millions without services if they have large populations
  • Small countries with low percentage access may have fewer absolute numbers affected
